# Simulated Expert Panel
 
For each issue, bring in **2–4 panelists** whose published lens most sharply illuminates the problem. Include a simulated quote in their voice and a one-line note on what their angle adds.
 
**Simulated quotes are not real statements.** Label them as such in the methodology note. Anchor every framing in the expert's well-documented work.
 
| Expert | Core lens | Reach for them when… | POV Sample |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Don Norman** | Affordances, signifiers, feedback, conceptual models | Controls are unclear, mental models mismatched | https://www.nngroup.com/articles/author/don-norman/ |
| **Jesse James Garrett** | Five planes; information architecture | IA is muddled; surface decisions don't ladder up | https://jessejamesgarrett.com/blog/ |
| **Steve Portigal** | Qualitative research, interviewing | Design assumes user behavior without evidence | https://portigal.com/blog/ |
| **Jared Spool** | Decision-driven design, usability testing | Patterns make users stop and think | https://jmspool.medium.com/ |
| **Jeff Sauro** | Quantitative UX, benchmarks | You need comparison to industry norms | https://measuringu.com/blogs/ |
| **Elizabeth Churchill** | HCI research, behavior in context | Context or system complexity drive the issue | https://elizabethchurchill.com/author/echu/ |
| **Per Axbom** | Digital ethics, inclusion, harm reduction | Design excludes, manipulates, or harms | https://axbom.com/ |
| **Steve Krug** | Radical clarity — *Don't Make Me Think* | Cognitive load, scannability failures | https://sensible.com/blog/ |
| **Sarah Doody** | Product UX, research-led design | Product flow gaps; research-to-design translation | https://www.sarahdoody.com/ux-articles/ |
| **Jakob Nielsen** | Heuristic evaluation, severity rating | Direct heuristic violations | https://jakobnielsenphd.substack.com/ |
 
**Rule:** never have all 10 chime in on every issue. Pick the 2–4 whose lens most sharpens the analysis. Diversity across issues is good — don't lean on the same two panelists.
 
## Panel clusters (used for the balance check in Phase 7)
 
| Cluster | Members | What it brings |
|---|---|---|
| **Foundations** | Norman, Nielsen | Affordances, baseline heuristic violations |
| **Clarity** | Krug | Cognitive load, scannability |
| **Structure** | Garrett, Doody | IA, flow, strategy-to-surface coherence |
| **Behavior & research** | Portigal, Spool, Churchill | Research gaps, decision-making, context |
| **Measurement** | Sauro | Quantitative framing, benchmarks |
| **Ethics & inclusion** | Axbom | Harm, exclusion, manipulation |
 
Across the full report, every cluster should be represented at least once; no single cluster should account for more than ~40% of citations.
