# Frameworks
 
## Nielsen's 10 Usability Heuristics
 
1. Visibility of system status
2. Match between system and the real world
3. User control and freedom
4. Consistency and standards
5. Error prevention
6. Recognition rather than recall
7. Flexibility and efficiency of use
8. Aesthetic and minimalist design
9. Help users recognize, diagnose, and recover from errors
10. Help and documentation

Full details: https://jakobnielsenphd.substack.com/p/the-10-usability-heuristics-reimagined?utm_source=publication-search

## Shneiderman's Eight Golden Rules
 
1. Strive for consistency
2. Seek universal usability
3. Offer informative feedback
4. Design dialogs to yield closure
5. Prevent errors
6. Permit easy reversal of actions
7. Keep users in control
8. Reduce short-term memory load

Full details: https://ixdf.org/literature/article/shneiderman-s-eight-golden-rules-will-help-you-design-better-interfaces

## Three buckets for what you find
 
Every candidate problem belongs in **one** of three buckets. Pick honestly — do not stretch a polish problem into a heuristic mapping just to keep it in the main catalog.
 
- **Heuristic Violation** — maps to at least one Nielsen heuristic or Shneiderman rule. Lives in the main Issue Catalog. Full schema.
- **Quality Issue** — a real defect that does not cleanly violate any heuristic (typos, polish problems, inconsistent capitalization). Lives in the main Issue Catalog tagged `Type: Quality Issue`. Replaces the heuristic-mapping fields with a `Why it matters` paragraph. Severity still applies.
- **Design Observation** — a suggestion, not a defect. Lives in the appendix.
